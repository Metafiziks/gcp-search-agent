Document Title: Finished Goods Defect Classification Standard
Document Type: Quality Control Standard
Department: Quality Assurance
Applies To: Production operators, floor supervisors, quality inspectors
Revision: 2026.03

Purpose:
This standard defines how finished goods defects are classified and handled before shipment.

Defect Classes:
Critical Defect:
A defect that could create a safety risk, regulatory issue, or product failure. Critical defects require immediate hold and Quality Assurance escalation.

Major Defect:
A defect that affects product function, fit, performance, customer usability, or appearance beyond approved limits. Major defects require supervisor review and possible rework or rejection.

Minor Defect:
A defect that does not affect function or customer use and remains within approved cosmetic limits. Minor defects may be accepted if documented.

Examples:
Critical defects include exposed sharp edges, missing safety labels, contamination, incorrect product configuration, or failed functional test.
Major defects include cracked housing, incorrect dimensions, incomplete assembly, missing non-safety label, or failed packaging seal.
Minor defects include small cosmetic scuffs, slight color variation within tolerance, or minor packaging blemish.

Containment:
If repeated defects are found, the supervisor must stop shipment of the affected lot, notify Quality Assurance, identify the last known good unit, and begin containment.

Rework:
Rework must follow approved work instructions. Reworked product must be reinspected before release.

Related Terms:
finished goods, defect classification, critical defect, major defect, minor defect, containment, rework, lot hold.
