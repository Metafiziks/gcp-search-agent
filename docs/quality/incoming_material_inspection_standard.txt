Document Title: Incoming Material Inspection Standard
Document Type: Quality Control Standard
Department: Quality Assurance
Applies To: Receiving, warehouse, production supervisors, quality inspectors
Revision: 2026.03

Purpose:
This standard defines the inspection process for raw materials, components, and packaging received from suppliers.

Inspection Requirements:
Incoming materials must be inspected before release to production. Inspection includes verification of supplier name, purchase order, lot number, certificate of analysis when required, quantity, packaging condition, and visible damage.

Sampling:
Quality inspectors must follow the approved sampling plan for each material category. Critical components require increased sampling frequency. Materials with prior supplier issues may require tightened inspection.

Acceptance Criteria:
Materials may be accepted if they match purchase specifications, show no contamination, include required documentation, and meet dimensional or visual requirements.

Rejection Criteria:
Materials must be rejected or quarantined if they are damaged, mislabeled, contaminated, missing required documentation, outside tolerance, or from an unapproved supplier.

Quarantine Process:
Rejected materials must be moved to the designated quarantine area and labeled with a hold tag. The material must not be used in production until Quality Assurance releases it.

Supervisor Responsibilities:
Floor supervisors must not use uninspected or quarantined materials to keep production running. If production is at risk, supervisors must escalate to Quality Assurance and Planning.

Related Terms:
incoming inspection, supplier quality, quarantine, certificate of analysis, lot number, sampling plan, material release.
