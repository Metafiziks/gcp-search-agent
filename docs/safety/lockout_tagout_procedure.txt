Document Title: Lockout Tagout Procedure
Document Type: Safety Procedure
Department: Plant Safety
Applies To: Floor supervisors, maintenance technicians, line operators
Revision: 2026.01

Purpose:
This procedure defines how employees must isolate hazardous energy before performing maintenance, cleaning, adjustment, or repair work on production equipment.

When Lockout Tagout Is Required:
Lockout tagout is required before any employee removes a guard, reaches into a machine, services powered equipment, clears a jam, replaces mechanical components, or performs maintenance where unexpected startup could cause injury.

Required Steps:
1. Notify affected employees that the equipment will be shut down.
2. Identify all energy sources, including electrical, pneumatic, hydraulic, thermal, and stored mechanical energy.
3. Shut down the equipment using the normal stop procedure.
4. Isolate the equipment from all energy sources.
5. Apply an approved lock and tag to each energy isolation point.
6. Release or restrain stored energy.
7. Verify zero energy by attempting to start the equipment using the normal controls.
8. Return controls to the off position after verification.

Supervisor Responsibilities:
Floor supervisors must confirm that lockout tagout is being followed before maintenance work begins. Supervisors may not remove another employee's lock unless the formal lock removal exception process is completed.

Exception Process:
If an employee who applied a lock is unavailable, the supervisor must contact Plant Safety, verify the employee is not on site, inspect the equipment, and document approval before removing the lock.

Important Safety Rule:
Never bypass lockout tagout to save production time. Production urgency does not override hazardous energy control requirements.

Related Terms:
LOTO, lockout, tagout, zero energy verification, hazardous energy, equipment isolation, machine guarding.
